INCENTIVES

FILM MAKING IN AUSTRALIA

Australia’s competitive Screen Production Incentives have enabled large budget feature films, TV shows and documentaries access to significant savings as well as Australia’s world class production services, crew, talent and versatile locations.

POST, DIGITAL AND VISUAL EFFECTS (PDV) OFFSET — The 30% PDV Offset (Post, Digital and Visual Effects) is a refundable tax offset (rebate) for Qualifying PDV Expenditure (QAPE) incurred in relation to post, digital and visual effects work.

THE LOCATION OFFSET —The Location Offset is a 16.5% refundable tax offset (rebate) calculated on Qualifying Australian Production Expenditure (QAPE). Also available is the Location Incentive Program offering funding of $140 million over 4 years. Funding of $35 million per annum will be provided each year commencing from 1 July 2019 to 30 June 2023.

THE PRODUCER OFFSET — The Producer Offset provides a refundable tax offset (rebate) for producers of Australian feature films, television and other projects on Qualifying Australian Production Expenditure (QAPE). Up to 40% for theatrically released feature films, documentary, animation and IMAX. Up to 20% for single episode dramas and documentary (including features released only on DVD or on-line), television drama or documentary series/seasons and short form animation.

STATE GOVERNMENT INCENTIVES

VICTORIA — Production Incentive Attraction Fund (PIAF) aims to attract ‘footloose’ film and television projects to Victoria. The minimum qualifying threshold is a Victorian spend of AUD $3.5 million. Development funding of up to $30,000 is also available, depending on the project type.

QUEENSLAND — Screen Queensland’s uncapped 10% Post, Digital & VFX Attraction Grant. The minimum qualifying threshold is a Queensland Post Production Spend of AUD $500,000. There is also the “SQ + Stan Premium Drama Development Fund” as well as SQ Market funding incentives.

SOUTH AUSTRALIA — Post Production, Digital and Visual Effects (PDV) Rebate the SAFC will rebate equivalent to 10% of expenditure on post production, digital and visual effects work undertaken in South Australia.

NEW SOUTH WALES — Film & Television Industry Attraction Program to attract productions. The attraction program is discretionary and incentives are provided in the form of rebates.
